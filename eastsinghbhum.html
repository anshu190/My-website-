INTRODUCTION

East Singhbhum is one of the most industrialized districts of Jharkhand, with Jamshedpur as its headquarters. Known as the "Steel City of India," Jamshedpur is home to Tata Steel, one of Asia's oldest and largest steel plants. The district is a unique blend of industrial development and natural beauty, with the Dalma Wildlife Sanctuary and picturesque rivers enhancing its landscape.

Jamshedpur - The Steel City of India

Tata Steel - Asia's First Integrated Steel Plant

Dalma Hills - Scenic Beauty and Wildlife Sanctuary

East Singhbhum is not just about industries; it's also rich in cultural heritage with diverse tribal communities, historical sites, and modern urban amenities. The district serves as a major economic hub while preserving its natural and cultural treasures.

GEOGRAPHY & CLIMATE

East Singhbhum covers an area of 3,533 square kilometers, characterized by hilly terrain, rivers, and urban landscapes.

AREA

3,533 sq km - Highly urbanized district

ELEVATION

100-900 meters above sea level

RIVERS

Subarnarekha, Kharkai, and Sanjai rivers flow through the district

CLIMATE

Humid subtropical climate with hot summers, monsoon rains, and mild winters

INDUSTRIAL SIGNIFICANCE

East Singhbhum is the industrial powerhouse of Jharkhand with major industries:

TATA STEEL

Asia's first integrated steel plant, established in 1907

TATA MOTORS

One of India's largest automobile manufacturing plants

OTHER INDUSTRIES

TIMET, Tayo, Tinplate, and numerous ancillary industries

ECONOMIC ZONE

HISTORICAL TIMELINE

Ancient Times

Region inhabited by indigenous tribes including Santhals and Hos

Medieval Period

Part of Singhbhum Kingdom and various tribal administrations

1907

Tata Steel established, marking the beginning of industrialization

1919

Jamshedpur officially established as a planned industrial city

1940s

Expansion of industrial activities with World War II demands

1990

Singhbhum district divided into East and West Singhbhum

2000

East Singhbhum became part of the new state of Jharkhand

Present

Continues to be a major industrial and economic hub of Eastern India
