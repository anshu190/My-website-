INTRODUCTION

Godda is a district in the northeastern part of Jharkhand, India, known for its rich mineral resources and industrial development. It was carved out of the former Santhal Parganas district and has emerged as an important economic center in the state, with significant coal reserves and power projects.

Industrial development in Godda

Located in the Santhal Parganas division, Godda is bordered by Bangladesh to the east and the state of Bihar to the north. The district is characterized by its diverse topography, ranging from lush forests to mineral-rich lands. Beyond its industrial significance, Godda also has cultural and historical importance with several tribal communities calling it home.

GEOGRAPHY & CLIMATE

Godda is situated at an average elevation of 277 meters above sea level. The district features a varied landscape with hills, forests, and plains.

AREA

Godda district covers 2,110 sq km with diverse topography

ELEVATION

277 meters above sea level

RIVERS

Several rivers including the Ganga, Bansloi, and Brahmani flow through the district

CLIMATE

Humid subtropical climate with hot summers (April-June), monsoon rains (July-September), and cool winters (November-February)
